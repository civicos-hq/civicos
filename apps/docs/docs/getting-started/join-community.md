# Join a community

Everything in CivicOS is scoped to a **community** — a state + Local
Government Area (LGA) pair. Issues, petitions, and representatives all
belong to one. Joining a community is what unlocks civic action for that
place.

## Your first community

When you land on CivicOS for the first time, you'll be prompted to pick
a community:

1. Choose your **state** (all 36 + FCT are listed).
2. Choose your **LGA** — the list narrows based on the state you picked.
3. Confirm.

Your first join sets **both** your primary community (see below) and
your active community.

## Primary vs. active community

CivicOS distinguishes two things:

- **Primary community** — your home constituency. This is the community
  where you can **create** issues, petitions, and (if you have the right
  role) representative profiles. Think of it as "where you actually live."
- **Active community** — the community you're currently viewing and
  acting in for signatures, comments, and upvotes.

Most of the time these are the same. They diverge when you visit another
community's page — you can still sign petitions and comment there, but
you can't create new issues outside your primary community.

## Joining another community

You can be a member of multiple communities. To join another:

1. Go to **Communities** in the top nav.
2. Find the community you want to join (search by name, state, or LGA).
3. Click **Join community**.

Joining doesn't change your primary — it just adds a new membership.

## Switching your active community

Use the community picker in the top nav to switch between communities
you've joined. Your feed, notifications badge, and the "act" buttons on
representative and issue pages all follow the active community.

## Changing your primary community

If you actually move — new city, new LGA — you can change your primary
community.

1. Go to **Profile → Community**.
2. Click **Change primary community**.
3. Pick the target (you must have already joined it).

**One caveat:** you can only change your primary community **once every
30 days**. This is deliberate — it stops people bouncing between
communities to stack signatures or file issues in places they don't
actually live. If you're within the 30-day cooldown, the UI shows the
next date you can change.
