# Responding to a consultation

A **consultation** is a structured question set that an organization
publishes to gather citizen input before making a decision — a
curriculum review, a policy change, a budget priority ask. Unlike a
petition (which is a binary "I support this"), a consultation is a form
you fill in, one response per person.

Every consultation on CivicOS goes through the same lifecycle:

- **Draft** — the organization is still preparing the questions. Not
  visible to citizens.
- **Published** — open for responses.
- **Closed** — no more responses accepted; the organization is reviewing.
- **Outcome published** — the organization has posted a summary of what
  citizens said, what they decided, and what happens next.

## Finding consultations

- The **Consultations** tab in the top nav shows every open consultation.
- Each consultation card shows the organization, the title, a short
  summary, and the closing date if one is set.
- Consultations you've already responded to are marked so you don't
  double-answer.

## Requirements to respond

- A **verified email** — same rule as commenting or signing petitions.
- Nothing else. Any verified user can respond to any consultation,
  regardless of which community they live in. Community tags on
  consultations are audience labels, not gates.

If a consultation is tagged for a community you're not part of, you can
still respond — but keep in mind the organization is looking primarily
for input from that community.

## Filling in the form

1. Open the consultation.
2. Read the **description** and any linked context.
3. Answer each question. Question types you'll see:
   - **Short text** — a sentence or two.
   - **Long text** — up to a few paragraphs.
   - **Single choice** — pick one option.
   - **Multi choice** — pick several options.
   - **Yes / No** — pick one.
4. Required questions are marked with `*`. You can't submit without
   answering them.
5. Click **Submit**.

Once you submit, your response is final for this consultation. There's
no unsubmit and no edit.

## Why one response per person

CivicOS enforces one response per verified account per consultation.
This is deliberate — if the same person can fill the form ten times, the
data is noise and the outcome is meaningless. Organizations rely on
that guarantee to make decisions.

Trying to submit twice returns an error: the response you already saved
stands.

## What happens after the consultation closes

- When the organization closes the consultation, you get a **notification**
  saying it's no longer accepting responses.
- When the organization publishes the **outcome**, you get another
  notification. This is the "close the loop" moment — a summary of what
  citizens said, the decisions the organization made, and the next
  steps.
- Outcome pages are public. Anyone can read them, forever. That's the
  point: the record of what happened after citizens participated.

## Frequently asked

**Are my answers anonymous?**
No. Your responses are tied to your account, same as your comments and
signatures. The organization sees who answered but the public-facing
outcome page only shows aggregated numbers, not individual responses.

**Can I see what other people answered?**
Not while the consultation is open. When the outcome is published, the
organization typically shares aggregated statistics ("62% said…").
Individual answers stay private.

**What if the questions are unfair or leading?**
Flag the consultation using the **⋯** menu. A moderator will review the
consultation itself, not your response.

**What if I disagree with the decisions the organization made?**
The outcome page is the record. If you disagree, use the other tools:
raise an issue, sign or create a petition, or comment on the
representative's page.
