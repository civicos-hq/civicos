# Notifications

CivicOS keeps you in the loop when something happens that concerns you —
a response to your issue, a milestone on your petition, or an update
from a representative you follow.

## Where to find them

Click the **🔔 bell icon** in the top nav. The number badge shows how
many unread notifications you have.

The dropdown shows your most recent items. Click a notification to open
the thing it refers to; clicking marks it as read.

## Realtime updates

Notifications arrive in **real time** while you have CivicOS open —
you'll see the bell badge tick up the moment something happens, no
refresh needed. This is powered by a persistent connection to the server
that stays alive for as long as your tab is open.

If you close the tab and come back later, unread notifications are still
there — nothing gets lost.

## What triggers a notification

You'll get notified when:

| Trigger                                                     | What you see                                                     |
| ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Someone comments on an issue you filed                      | "New comment on your issue"                                      |
| An issue you filed changes status                           | "Issue status updated" — includes the new status                 |
| Someone comments on a petition you started                  | "New comment on your petition"                                   |
| Someone signs your petition                                 | "New signature on your petition" — with the current count / goal |
| Your petition hits 25 %, 50 %, or 100 % of its goal         | "Petition milestone: 50%"                                        |
| A representative you follow posts an official response      | "New response from &lt;name&gt;"                                 |
| Your representative or organization application is reviewed | "Application approved" / "Needs changes" / "Rejected"            |
| An organization publishes a new consultation                | "New consultation: &lt;title&gt;"                                |
| A consultation you responded to closes                      | "Consultation closed: &lt;title&gt;"                             |
| A consultation you responded to has its outcome published   | "Outcome published: &lt;title&gt;"                               |

## What doesn't notify you

To keep the noise down, CivicOS **doesn't** notify you when:

- Random citizens comment on a representative's page you also follow —
  only **official responses** trigger a notification.
- You're the person doing the action — you don't get pinged for your own
  comment, upvote, or signature.
- An issue in your community is filed by someone else — check the feed
  or the discover page for that.

## Managing your notifications

- **Mark all as read** — click the button at the top of the bell
  dropdown.
- **Mark one as read** — clicking the notification opens the target and
  marks it read automatically.
- **See everything** — go to **Profile → Notifications** for the full
  list (up to 200).

## Deep links

Every notification carries a deep link to the exact place the event
happened — the specific comment, the milestone timeline, the
representative's response section. Click and go straight there.
